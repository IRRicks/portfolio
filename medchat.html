MedChat summary

My goal was to take the current patient portal applications on the market, and redesign them to create a more user friendly experience.

View finished prototype

Presenting Problem:

Current patient portal applications feature homepages that are cluttered, confusing and fail to prioritize user flows.

The Solution:



Discovery Phase:

Before beginning the design process, I needed to gather information about how users felt about their current patient portal applications, the way they use them, and the features they would most like to see in future applications.

View more

Competitive Analysis:

I conducted an analysis on three popular applications in the patient portal market: MyChart, FollowMyHealth and healow. I found that an opportunity exists in this market for an application that could take some of the most popular features of the MyChart application and cut out some of the unnecessary features.

View more

User Personas:

I was able to craft user personas based on the information gathered in the survey. Demographically, the respondents were majority female (80%), and aged between 25-54 (80%).

Preference Tests:

I conducted a preference test for three different homepage designs. I was curious which layout users preferred.

Overall, the majority of users indicated a preference for homepage #2.

Branding and Identity:

The overall style of MedChat is one that projects reliability, trustworthiness, and friendliness. As a patient-facing application, we want consumers to feel comfortable while using our product.

Usability Tests:

I conducted testing on my first round of high fidelity mockups using maze.design to understand if there were any issues that needed to be addressed before moving forward.


I found that users were having trouble with navigating the log-in page and the inbox/messages.

I decided that I needed to address these issues in my next version of the design.
